Anxiety

 Anxiety is a basically a feeling of fear, nervousness and uneasiness. It has affected nearly 30% of adults in their lives. It may be caused by overthinking or when we face some difficult situations in life like making an important decision, given a difficult task at work, being scolded very badly by someone etc. Anxiety affects more than 284 million people in the world. Women are twice as likely to be affected than men. Crying without any reason, suffocation, fast heart rate, sweating, feeling tired all the time, feeling nauseous or rapid breathing may occur. Anxiety disorder, Social anxiety disorder, Separation anxiety and Obsessive compulsive disorder can be treated medically or with self-treatment.Anxiety is a common mental disorder which can be caused by difficult experiences in childhood, teenage or adulthood. Sometimes, these experiences can act as a trigger for anxiety problems. If it is not taken care of, it can get worse with time. Anxiety makes it difficult to get through your day, struggling to do even daily activities like eating. Approximately 3.5% of the world’s population suffers from anxiety, of which 63% are females and 37% are males. Many anxiety disorders cause people to experience panic attacks which are periods of intense fear triggered by an object or situation that can reach their limit within minutes or even seconds. According to Jill Stoddard, a psychologist based in San Diego, avoidance of the triggers of anxiety is common ground for all anxiety disorders. If you seek medical help, then a single test can’t diagnose anxiety. Instead, it requires a lengthy process of physical examinations, mental health tests, and psychological questionnaires. Once you’ve received a diagnosis of anxiety, you can receive treatment from a doctor as treatment can help you overcome the symptoms, recognize your triggers and lead a more manageable, convenient and happy day-to-day life. Meeting with a therapist or psychologist can help you to learn and use strategies and tools to cope with stress and panic attacks when they occur. You can use self-treatment by practicing following activities- Meditation,getting enough sleep, exercising regularly , practicing yoga, eating a healthy diet ,avoiding alcohol, caffeine and cigarettes as much as possible, focusing on your breathing ,focusing on the present moment only, drinking lots and lots of water, doing something you enjoy, going where you feel peaceful, using positive self-talks and relaxing. Anxiety disorders are related to suicide attempts as it leads to suicidal thoughts and feeling of escapism from the worldly situations. But, we need to remember that suicide is not an option as our lives matter and they are very precious to us and our near and dear ones. If you are feeling anxious, stressful and in general, down on some days, you should always talk to someone whom you are comfortable with. Your mental health is important and you should not try to hide your feelings and put up a mask of happiness on your face. Remember, you just need to talk and there are always people there to help you and who want to see you happy and this is just a phase of life which will pass and you are always stronger than you think you are.

Symptoms

Feeling nervous, restless or tense, feeling tired all the time, trouble in concentrating in the present etc. are the common symptoms of anxiety.

Causes

Traumatic incidents in childhood, adolescence or adulthood may be the leading cause of anxiety. Otherwise, it depends on many factors.

Prevention And 
Treatment

Almost all types of anxieties are treatable either by medical treatment or self-treatment.
